# Beta Partner Brief: Bukeer Human-Agent Operating System

> Date: 2026-05-18
> Audience: beta partner decision makers, agency managers and lead planners
> Phase: 0.25

## What Is Changing

Bukeer Admin is being redesigned from a traditional admin system into a human-agent travel operating system.

This does not mean replacing travel agents with AI. It means giving agents and managers a workspace where AI can help prepare work, but humans keep control of customer communication, pricing, approvals and sensitive actions.

## Why This Matters

Travel agencies lose time because key work is fragmented:

- Leads arrive in conversations.
- Customer preferences live in chat history.
- Itinerary planning happens somewhere else.
- Product and supplier data are separate.
- Pricing and margin checks are manual.
- Managers approve decisions after context has already been lost.

The new direction puts these pieces into a single operating flow.

## The First Priority Experience

The first priority is **Planner Workbench**.

It brings together:

- Lead and conversation context.
- Trip brief.
- Missing customer data.
- AI-suggested reply.
- AI-suggested itinerary draft.
- Product and supplier options.
- Margin and risk checks.
- Manager approval when needed.
- Trace of what the AI used and proposed.

## What AI Will Do

AI can:

- Summarize conversations.
- Identify missing data.
- Suggest replies.
- Draft itinerary ideas.
- Compare product options.
- Flag margin or policy risks.
- Prepare actions for human review.
- Explain why it is suggesting something.

## What AI Will Not Do By Default

AI should not silently:

- Send public customer messages.
- Confirm bookings.
- Execute payments.
- Override margin policy.
- Reserve supplier holds.
- Change confirmed financial data.
- Send mass outreach.
- Perform destructive platform actions.

These actions require explicit human approval, stronger permissions or later-phase policy decisions.

## Ontologia B1 Explained

Ontologia B1 is the shared business vocabulary for Bukeer.

It defines core travel concepts such as:

- Lead
- Traveler
- Contact
- Conversation
- Itinerary
- Quote
- Offer
- Booking
- Supplier
- Product
- Rate
- Availability
- Policy
- Payment
- Voucher
- Work Item

The value is simple: humans, screens, services and AI agents use the same language. That reduces confusion and makes workflows traceable.

## Benefits For Users

For travel agents:

- Faster response to leads.
- Less context switching.
- Better itinerary drafts.
- Clear missing-data checklist.
- AI suggestions that can be edited before use.

For managers:

- Approval queue with impact and risk.
- Better visibility into SLA, margin and blocked actions.
- Traceability for AI-assisted work.
- Safer rollout of automation.

For the agency:

- More consistent planning process.
- Better control of margin and policy.
- Less dependency on individual memory.
- Foundation for future agentic workflows without giving up governance.

## Risks And How We Control Them

Risk: AI sends wrong information.

Control: AI suggestions are drafts. Humans review before public send in the initial phase.

Risk: AI takes unsafe actions.

Control: sensitive actions are blocked or approval-required.

Risk: users do not trust AI.

Control: every suggestion shows rationale, data used, confidence, risk and trace.

Risk: the UI becomes too complex.

Control: Planner Workbench is designed around daily workflows, not a generic dashboard.

Risk: automation is rolled out too early.

Control: autonomy levels start read-only/suggest first, then draft, then confirmed write only by policy.

## What We Need From Beta Partners

Review and validate:

- Does Planner Workbench match how planners actually work?
- Are the missing-data and approval states clear?
- Would agents trust and edit AI suggestions?
- What actions should never be automated?
- What manager approvals are truly needed?
- Which fields matter most for quote speed, margin and customer confidence?

## Success Criteria

The beta is successful when:

- A planner can move from conversation to itinerary draft faster.
- The planner understands what AI proposed and why.
- Managers can review risky actions with enough context.
- No sensitive action happens without the right human control.
- The workflow feels like agency operations, not a generic SaaS dashboard.
