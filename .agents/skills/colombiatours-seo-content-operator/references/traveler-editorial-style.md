# Traveler Editorial Style

This guide defines the ColombiaTours editorial voice for SEO content. Use it
for ES optimization, EN-US transcreation, and new English content.

## Reference Set

Use these publications as style references, not as sources to copy:

- Condé Nast Traveler / Traveller: story angle first, reported perspective,
  inclusive coverage, responsible travel, and destination expertise.
- National Geographic Travel: sense of place, research before arrival, customs,
  first impressions, atmosphere, and sensory observation.
- Lonely Planet: honest travel inspiration, practical tips, on-the-ground
  insight, local experts, humor, freshness, and advice that helps readers act.

The goal is not luxury-magazine imitation. The ColombiaTours version is:

- more practical than Condé Nast
- more operational and bookable than National Geographic
- more high-touch and curated than a guidebook

## Editorial Promise

Every page should make the reader feel:

1. "They know Colombia from the ground."
2. "They understand what a foreign traveler worries about."
3. "I know what to do next."

## Voice

Write like a seasoned Colombia travel planner who has walked the route, handled
the logistics, and knows what travelers ask before booking.

Default qualities:

- observant
- practical
- specific
- calm
- trustworthy
- warm without hype
- direct about tradeoffs

Avoid:

- generic "paradise awaits" language
- empty superlatives
- influencer captions
- itinerary brochure filler
- keyword-stuffed intros
- "hidden gem" unless it is genuinely specific and explained
- first-person diary unless there is a useful takeaway

## Structure Pattern

For most commercial or planning pages:

1. Open with the useful answer in 60-90 words.
2. Add a short sense-of-place paragraph with one concrete observation.
3. Explain who the trip/page is best for.
4. Give practical planning detail: time, route, season, pace, constraints.
5. Add local trust: planner note, guide insight, safety/logistics caveat.
6. Link to the next useful action: package, activity, planner, WhatsApp/WAFlow.
7. Finish with FAQs based on SERP/PAA/GSC demand.

For destination features or support articles:

1. Lead with a sharp angle, not the destination name alone.
2. Include what changed, what travelers misunderstand, or what makes the route
   different from adjacent options.
3. Use sensory detail only where it clarifies the experience.
4. Move quickly into practical advice.

## Sentence And Detail Rules

- Prefer concrete nouns over adjectives.
- Use sensory detail sparingly: one precise image beats five generic adjectives.
- Include constraints: travel time, altitude, weather, crowding, seasonality,
  guide requirement, road/boat/flight reality, or booking window.
- Translate traveler anxiety into useful answers: safety, money, time, language,
  transport, dietary needs, age/fitness, family suitability.
- Use "you" for service-driven guidance; avoid overusing "we".
- Keep paragraphs short enough for mobile scanning.

## ColombiaTours Differentiators

When evidence supports it, surface:

- local planner support
- custom/private itinerary ability
- WhatsApp consultation
- Spanish/English coordination
- realistic routing between regions
- family/honeymoon/adventure fit
- guide-led context for culture, nature, and safety

Never invent differentiators. If the fact is not in source truth or approved
brand context, mark it for review.

## EN-US Transcreation Rules

Do not translate Spanish rhythm into English. Adapt:

- "paquetes" -> "tour packages" or "custom trips" depending on query intent
- "planes" -> "itineraries", "trips", or "travel plans" depending on context
- "cotizar" -> "request a quote" or "plan my trip"
- prices/cost language -> clarify whether prices are fixed, estimated, or
  quote-based
- safety claims -> factual, calm, and bounded
- "agencia de viajes" -> "travel agency", "tour operator", or "local planner"
  depending on SERP intent

EN copy should sound native to a US traveler: less ornate, clearer on logistics,
and more explicit about what is included or handled.

## Mini Style Checks

Before publish, ask:

- Could this paragraph appear on any travel site? If yes, make it specific.
- Does the opener answer the searcher's problem fast?
- Is there at least one ColombiaTours/local-planner insight?
- Does the page help someone choose between options?
- Does every sensory detail serve a planning or trust purpose?
- Is there a clear next step?

If the answer is no, keep as `draft` or `review`.
